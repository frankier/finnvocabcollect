The study

The study consists of a self-assessment followed by a mini-exam.

In the self-assessment, you will self-rate your familiarity with Finnish words on a 5-point scale. In the mini-exam, you will be further tested on your knowledge of a few Finnish words.

The self-assessment scale

The self-assessment scale is 5-point scale explained below:

  • Your choice on the scale says 2 things:
    • Whether you think you have seen the word
    • Whether you think you know the meaning of the word
  • Point 1 on scale means you have not seen the word
  • Point 3 on the scale means you have seen the word, but do not know the meaning
  • Point 5 on the scale means you know the meaning of the word
  • Points 2 and 4 can be used if you are unsure about which of their neighbours to choose

For a compound word, you might know what the word means even though you have never enountered it before. In this case you should select 4 or 5 depending on your confidence. However, if you know all the parts of the compound word, but do not know their meaning together, then you should select 1 or 2 if you think you have never encountered the compound before.

Planning to complete the study in time

The self-assessment is estimated to take between 8 and 16 hours, with 12 hours being typical. This means you should expect to complete it over more than 1 day. After this, about 30 minutes should be reserved for the mini-exam.

Please think about whether you are able to commit to completing this before the deadline shown in the top right before agreeing to participate. Here are 2 scenarios for how it could be completed:

  1. Participant A has 2 spare days to complete the assessment. On the first day they complete 3 hours of the self-assessment in the morning, and then take a break for lunch. They then complete 3 hours in the afternoon. On the second day they do the same, and complete the mini-exam in the evening.
  2. Participant B is busy during the day Monday to Friday, but has some spare time in the evening and at weekends. They complete 2 hours every evening during Monday to Friday and complete a final 2 hours as well as the mini-exam on Saturday.

If you are not able to fit it into your schedule, please withdraw from the study at the bottom of this page.

Prolonged screen usage can be dangerous for your eyes. Please adjust your screen brightness to match your surroundings and take a 10 minute screen break every hour.
